1654 Oath Book

The 1654 Prysiazhni knyhy, or Oath Books, preserve one of the clearest mid-seventeenth-century attestations of the Zaichyk surname: Trofymka Zaichyk, listed among the townspeople of Stol'ne.

The Source

A published oath-book volume for the Bila Tserkva and Nizhyn regiments.

Prysiazhni knyhy · 1654 · Bila Tserkva and Nizhyn regiments

ПРИСЯЖНІ КНИГИ 1654 р.

The oath books record names from communities that swore allegiance in 1654. In the Nizhyn-regiment material, the surname appears in the section for the villages of Stol'ne, under the heading for townspeople.

The published volume is important here as the source context for the entry. The actual Zaichyk attestation appears inside the list, while the cover simply identifies the printed edition of the Prysiazhni knyhy being used.

The Zaichyk Entry

Trofymka Zaichyk appears in the Stol'ne townspeople list.

Page 142 · villages of Stol'ne · townspeople

Трофимка Зайчик

On page 142, under the heading СЕЛА СТОЛЕНСКОГО and the social category Мещане, the list includes Трофимка Зайчик. The name appears in a column of local townspeople, placing the surname in a civic community context in 1654.

Translation

Villages of Stol'ne. Townspeople: ... Trofymka Zaichyk ...

The entry is brief, but historically important. It places the surname in a dated Cossack-era register and identifies the bearer as part of the townspeople category rather than as an undifferentiated later parish entry.

Context from the Entry

The oath-book entry is brief, but the source preserves useful date, place, and social context.

The entry for Trofymka Zaichyk appears in a formal 1654 oath-book volume, under the section for the villages of Stol'ne and the heading Мещане, or townspeople. This places the surname in a dated civic-administrative source from the Cossack-era record tradition.

The social label is important. Мещане identifies the listed individuals as townspeople rather than simply recording them as undifferentiated names. In this context, the entry connects Trofymka Zaichyk to the local civic community of Stol'ne.

The form in the source, Трофимка Зайчик, also shows the surname in a straightforward Cyrillic spelling. Unlike the Dubno testaments, where the surname appears across Ruthenian and Polish written forms, this entry preserves the name in a compact East Slavic administrative list.
