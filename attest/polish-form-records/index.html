Record Context

These entries preserve the Latin-script form of the surname in western record environments.

The records on this page show the surname in the form Zajczyk, the Polish orthographic equivalent of the Cyrillic Зайчик. The spelling reflects the language and script of the records rather than a separate surname.

The Lviv entries belong to the Galician record world of the Habsburg Monarchy, where Roman Catholic parish registers commonly used Latin formats and Polish name forms. The Oshmiany district record shows the same surname transcribed from Latin into Cyrillic in a western Belarusian borderland context.
